Chelsea FC had finally climbed the mountain again in 2021. WIth a 1-0 win over Manchester City on a faithful night in Wembly, the Pride of London retook their throne as Champions of Europe. With a solid core, a great coach in Tomas Tuchel, and the full support of the ownership, they seemed to be untouchable. The thing that brought them down… the Rusian Invasion of Ukraine.

You might be wondering how that could possibly bring an English football team from the top of the mountain to a staggering 12th place the next season. After the invasion started in 2022, the English FA forced Russian billionare Ruben Abromivich, the owner of Chelsea to sell to Clearlake Captial and Todd Bohley, a group that would soon be known as Blueco.

Todd and Clearlake had an idea. This idea was a project to build a dynasty for years to come. They purchased about every player under the sun, however, they were all under age 25. Managers found this contrevercial due to lack of experience, however it was the decision of Blueco at the end of the day.

As we enter year 4 of the Chelsea project, are on their 6th manager, billions spent in transfer fees, and no leauge titles or champions leauges to show for it.

Lets dive into some of the numbers from their championship winning year versus recent years to identify where the glaring holes are to see if money can patch them starting with goal differential:
